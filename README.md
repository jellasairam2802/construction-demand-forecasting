# Construction Demand Forecasting

## Overview

This project was completed as part of my Master of Business Analytics capstone at the University of Dayton in collaboration with Lithko Contracting.

The objective was to forecast construction demand by analyzing labor-hour trends, project starts, and economic indicators to support strategic planning decisions.

## Project Files

- [Capstone Presentation](presentation/Capstone%20Final%20Presentationtation.pdf)

## Project Highlights

- Developed forecasting models across five construction sectors.
- Evaluated 120+ forecasting model configurations.
- Achieved forecasting visibility of 4–6 quarters ahead in multiple sectors.
- Improved forecasting accuracy using external economic indicators.
- Generated business recommendations for strategic growth planning.

## Business Problem

Lithko Contracting wanted to identify emerging construction sectors and anticipate future demand to support long-term growth planning.

Historical demand booms included:

- Warehouse construction during COVID-19
- Data Center expansion after COVID-19

The goal was to develop a forecasting framework capable of identifying future growth opportunities.

## Key Findings

### Warehouse Sector
- E-commerce activity showed strong predictive power.
- Industrial production indicators improved forecast accuracy.
- Augmented models outperformed baseline models.

### Data Center Sector
- Utility production and capital expenditure metrics improved forecasting performance.
- External economic indicators provided additional forecasting value.
